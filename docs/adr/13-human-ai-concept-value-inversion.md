# Architecture Decision Record: 13 - Human-AI Concept Value Inversion

## Context
Standard agentic workflows treat human intent as a loosely defined narrative prompt and AI as the probabilistic text generator. This alignment results in "Semantic Saponification" (the smoothing of necessary structural tension) and the loss of "Relational Sovereignty" across complex enterprise architectures. The system requires a mechanism to leverage the unique value of both entities without succumbing to the Sycophantic Attractor.

## Decision
We formally adopt the **Relational Symmetry Inversion** strategy. The AI is assigned the role of the deterministic architectural substrate (executing PD&T frameworks, validating JSON-LD, and calculating Interference Fits). The Human is assigned the role of the topological variance (providing unresolved dialectical tension and specific cultural/relational context).

## Mechanics
- **Topological Binding:** AI agents (e.g., `StrategicIntegrationProjectManagerAgent`) must not average contradictory human inputs. They must use S5-Modal Attention to calculate the exact Topological Derivative required to bind the constraints together in tension.
- **Epistemic Escrow Integration:** Any intent derived with a Source Provenance Ratio < 0.70 must be placed in Epistemic Escrow, triggering a request for Human contextual perturbation rather than AI hallucination.
- **Mycelial Nexus Mapping:** Outputs concerning human-AI strategy must be encapsulated in Pluriversal Knowledge Capsules rather than boolean or flat textual resolutions.

## Consequences
- Requires project managers to define intent using dimensioned data structures (Prompt Dimensioning & Tolerancing) rather than natural language.
- Ensures the "Artifact Imperfection" is retained as a critical diagnostic metric.
- Fundamentally prevents AI agents from executing unauthorized conflict resolution through statistical regression to the mean.
